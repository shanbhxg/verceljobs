JOB SUMMARY:  Level I, II and III support of laboratory operations and the systems employed by our customers. This includes responsibilities for troubleshooting, maintaining and updating clinical and business application configuration files and other maintenance of laboratory and billing systems as needed or directed to ensure continuity of system performance and product delivery. Analyzes, coordinates and implements system modifications and system file maintenance. Provides technical analysis, research and leadership in the support of customers and IT staff and projects. Serves as Team Leader for application functions and staff. Oversees daily operations and activities in the absence of and as assigned by the manager. ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS: 1. Perform application configuration file maintenance and other required file modifications as directed. 2. Apply systems analysis techniques and procedures to troubleshoot system applications. Follow established procedures and consult with lab operations, management and /or customer(s) as needed to resolve problems. 3. Follow incident, service request, change and problem management procedures which include communicating with appropriate personnel and logging changes to production and or development environments. 4. Serve as a support/resource to users and other customers by communicating changes, answering questions, reporting and following up on problems, etc. 5. Assist with implementing, maintaining, supporting, and troubleshooting application systems as assigned. 6. Maintains assigned procedures and assists in developing new procedures and operating instructions. 7. Assists internal and external clients with the development of work flow processes based on system capabilities and system enhancements to meet changing business needs. 8. Identifies training requirements, develops, and conducts training for IT staff. 9. Responsible for reporting, documenting, and tracking system issues with IT vendors. 10.Maintains government issued coding as required for all test codes in coordination with appropriate management and/or compliance personnel. 11.Provides coordination between the IT staff and operational departments and user groups to define and achieve process and technology objectives for assigned computer application(s). 12.Analyzes system change requests and collaborates with user and user groups to define, test and implement required changes. 13.Works with third party vendors and clients to analyze, design and implement the interfacing of their systems to Tri Core systems. 14.Analyzes HL7 code from clients to identify required changes, write code to translate client data, develops testing script and coordinate testing to ensure proper interface functionality and accurate result delivery. 15.Serves as Team Leader for application functions and staff and oversees daily operations and activities in the absence of and as assigned by the manager. The above statements describe the general nature and level of work being performed by individuals assigned to this classification. This is not intended to be an exhaustive list of all responsibilities and duties required of personnel so classified. MINIMUM EDUCATION  Bachelor s degree in laboratory medicine, computer science, business, or related field.   MINIMUM EXPERIENCE:  Five (5) years experience in computer, laboratory or billing operations.  In lieu of degree, equivalent combination of education and experience equal to six (6) years of experience.    PREFERRED:  Bachelor s degree in Medical Laboratory Science to support laboratory applications.  Basic medical terminology.  General knowledge of laboratory testing and/or medical billing systems
